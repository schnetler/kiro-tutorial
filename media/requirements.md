# Creating requirements.md

The `requirements.md` file is the foundation of your project. It answers: **"What should this software do?"**

## What Goes in requirements.md?

### Functional Requirements
Features and capabilities your software must have:
- "User must be able to login with email and password"
- "System must validate email format"
- "User sessions must persist for 24 hours"

### Non-Functional Requirements
Performance, security, and quality attributes:
- "Login response time must be under 2 seconds"
- "System must support 1000 concurrent users"
- "All passwords must be encrypted at rest"

### Constraints
Limitations and boundaries:
- Browser compatibility requirements
- Compliance needs (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)
- Budget and timeline constraints

### Out of Scope
What you're NOT building (yet):
- Future features planned for later phases
- Explicitly excluded functionality
